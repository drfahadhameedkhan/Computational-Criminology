# Chapter 2 exercises

## Designing a computational study

- Write a one-page preregistration for the question “did burglary concentrate more tightly at micro-places in city X after 2020?” using street-level open data from data.police.uk or an equivalent portal in your own jurisdiction. State the unit of analysis, the outcome, the comparison, and the analysis you will run, before downloading anything.
- Take the abstracts of three published quantitative criminology papers. For each, identify the unit of analysis, the level at which the claim is made, and whether the two match. Flag any inference that crosses levels.
- Choose a dataset you have access to and list five design decisions that its structure forecloses. For example, data aggregated to districts forecloses street-level questions; data without dates forecloses temporal ones.
- Download one month of incident data for a city of your choice and write a short memo describing its sampling frame: what process put a record into this file, what kinds of event could never appear in it, and what population your analysis of it could honestly claim to describe.

---

Coding tasks above can be started from the matching chapter script (`code/python/ch02_*.py` or `code/r/ch02_*.R`) and the synthetic data in `data/synthetic/`. Where an exercise asks for real data, the data directory README lists open sources you can substitute.
