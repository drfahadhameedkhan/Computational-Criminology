# Chapter 1 exercises

## The shape of computational criminology

- Choose a recent empirical paper that describes itself as computational criminology, predictive policing research, or crime data science. Identify which of the four question types it answers, which strand of the field it belongs to, and whether the method chosen matches the question asked. Write your assessment in no more than 500 words.
- Quetelet wrote that society could almost predict its annual budget of crime in advance. State the assumption about recorded crime that this claim rests on, and assess whether the assumption holds for one modern dataset you name.
- Find a media report about an “AI that predicts crime”. Identify the question type the underlying tool actually answers and the question type the report implies it answers. Note every point at which prediction is presented as if it were explanation.
- Write a one-paragraph research question about crime in a city or region you know well. State whether it would be best pursued through data analysis, through simulation, or through a combination, and justify the choice in terms of what each strand can and cannot establish.
