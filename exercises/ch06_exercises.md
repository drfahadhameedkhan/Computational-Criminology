# Chapter 6 exercises

## Ethics, privacy, and law

- Take an open incident-level crime dataset that includes small geographic areas and offence types. Without writing any code, describe how an adversary holding one piece of outside knowledge could re-identify a victim or suspect, and identify which published fields make it possible.
- Write the ethics section for the data-collection exercise you completed in Chapter 4, covering consent, re-identification, group harm, retention, and the terms of use of the source.
- Compare the research provisions of the GDPR with the data protection law of your own jurisdiction. Identify one respect in which each regime is stricter than the other for a crime researcher.
- You have built a tool that maps co-offending networks from arrest records. Write a dual-use memo: who else could use this tool, for what, and what safeguards in design or release would reduce the risk without destroying the research value.

---

Coding tasks above can be started from the matching chapter script (`code/python/ch06_*.py` or `code/r/ch06_*.R`) and the synthetic data in `data/synthetic/`. Where an exercise asks for real data, the data directory README lists open sources you can substitute.
